About Me

Hey! I'm Mehrab, a Computer Science & Engineering graduate from BRAC University (BSc CSE, Feb 2026). I'm a versatile technologist who has studied and worked across a wide spectrum — from software development and AI to robotics, networks, databases, and compiler design.

I'm not narrowly focused. I enjoy solving real problems, whether that means writing code, leading a team, thinking through a business challenge, or designing a system from scratch. I've built full-stack web apps, worked as a robotic engineer, conducted security research, and competed internationally.

My thesis was on code obfuscation — building a tool that protects software source code from reverse engineering. Outside academics, I've been involved in MUN, the Duke of Edinburgh Award, BYLC leadership training, and won awards at international innovation competitions.

Outside of tech, I cook, practice taekwondo, play table tennis, and have built a few browser-based games as personal projects — not a career goal, just something I enjoy tinkering with.
