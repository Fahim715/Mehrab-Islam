About Me

Hey! I'm Mehrab, a Computer Science & Engineering graduate from BRAC University (BSc CSE, Feb 2026). I'm a versatile technologist who has studied and worked across a wide spectrum—from software development and AI to robotics, networks, databases, and compiler design.

I'm not narrowly focused — I enjoy exploring and solving problems across different domains. I've tinkered with full-stack web apps, dabbled in robotics, dug into security research, and competed internationally. I'm still learning, but I'm the kind of person who dives in headfirst and figures things out along the way.

My thesis, supervised by Professor Muhammad Nur Yanhaona, was on "Multithreading for obfuscating code against dynamic analysis and reverse engineering" — an experience I'm truly grateful for.

Outside academics, I've participated in MUN, the Duke of Edinburgh Award, and won awards at international competitions. I also took part in BBLT33 (Building Bridges through Leadership Training 2021) hosted by BYLC, under the mentorship of Sabrina Hasan Rakhi, who played a big role in shaping who I am today.

Outside of tech, I cook, practice taekwondo, play table tennis, and have built a few browser-based games as personal projects — not a career goal, just something I enjoy tinkering with.
